6 WEEKS

    Oral Polio Vaccine (OPV) - 1


    1.This is the first OPV dose taken at 6 weeks. The next dose is taken when your child is is 10 weeks old, and the last dose at 14 weeks old.

    2.Administered orally.

    3.This vaccine protects against the poliovirus which is a highly infectious disease that invades the nervous system and can lead to total paralysis.

    4.The virus primarily affects children 5 years and below.

    Potential side effects of this vaccine include:

    There are no common side effects associated with this vaccine.

    Pentavalent - 1


    1.This is the first dose taken at 6 weeks old.
    2.The next dose is taken when your child is 10 weeks old and the last dose at 14 weeks old.
    3.Administered via injection
    4.This vaccine offers protection against Diphtheria, Pertussis, Tetanus, Hepatitis B and Hib.

    Potential side effects of this vaccine include:

    5.Swelling, redness and pain may occur at the site where the injection is given.
    6.Children may develop fever for a short time after immunization.
    7.Symptoms usually appear the day after vaccination and last between 1-3 days.

    Rotavirus Vaccine (RVV) - 1

    1.This is the first dose of three doses. The second dose is taken when your child is 10 weeks old and the last dose at 14 weeks old.
    2.Administered orally.
    3.This vaccine offers protection against rotaviruses which are the most common cause of severe diarrhoeal disease in infants and young chilren.

    Potential side effects of this vaccine include:

    4.Side effects are rare and mild.
    5.May include diarrhea, vomiting and irritation
